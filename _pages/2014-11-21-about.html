Hi! Welcome to my small blog, so glad you found your way here. I'm Dario and I run this blog.

I started blogging as a little hobby way back when in 2013 or 2014. I wrote about my experience with my first professional VMware bootcamp training. Looking back this was the kickstarter for my career in IT where I focus on VMware datacenter technology.

My career path started out where I finished my apprenticeship. I worked for four years in the internal IT of a big swiss retail firm and was handling the entire VMware stack of that company. The VMware infrastructure had grown so much in my four years there. I think we started out with around 20 ESXi hosts and when I left four years later we had 180 of them stretched across two datacenters.

Afterwards my path took me to the partner side where I focused even more on VMware technology and also wrote my first ESXi staging appliance which is basically a small linux with a php frontend for a dhcp/pxeserver to stage and configure ESXi hosts. I stayed in that position for four more years. At one point I felt like I had to take up a new challenge. The company I was with went through a merger where most of my colleagues left over the course of two years.

Recently, in may of 2019, I started my new position at BUSINESS IT AG in switzerland. My job is basically the same, I am part of the Professional Services team with a very strong focus on VMware technologies. However the new challenge here is that I will take up the technical leadership mantle for the entire automation stack. So basically vRealize Orchestrator in conjunction with vRealize Automation.

I am very glad for this opportunity and will be sharing as much as possible about the things that I learn now.

Another goal of mine is to achieve a VCDX certification. However I overestimated myself and underestimated the VCDX path at the same time and was so overwhelmed by it that my efforts in that regard came to a full stop. I will for sure give it another go but first I want to focus on my new role and the life beside the job.

Please feel free to reach out to me if you have any questions or feedback or just want to tell me that my blog sucks. I blog to provide value and to give back to the community from which I have gained so much so if you feel like I should do something different just let me know. Any feedback is better than none from my point of view.

Looking forward to hear from you.
